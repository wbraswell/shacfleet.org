6. Sample Duties of Adult Leaders

Skipper (Chief Operating Officer)
Serve as the key adult leader of a Sea Scout ship.
Give direction to ship's program.
Advise and coach the officers as they plan, organize and conduct the meetings and activities of the ship.
Be a liaison between the adult leaders, the chartered organization, and the youth.
Attend ship, quarterdeck, activity and ship committee meetings.
Keep informed of district, council, regional, and national activities of Sea Scouts.
Show a personal interest in all members.
 
Mate - Administration
Assist the Skipper as directed.
Advise and help the boatswain's mate with ceremonies.
Advise and help the boatswain's mate recruit new members.
Assumes the Skipper's duties in his or her absence.
 
Mate - Program
Assist the Skipper as directed.
Advise and help the boatswain’s mate perform the program duties.
Advise and help the boatswain's mate conduct interest surveys.
Advise and help the boatswain's mate compile an activity file.
Ship Committee
Select and recruit the adult leaders.
Provide facilities for the ship meeting place.
Supervise ship funds and property and help obtain supplies and equipment.
See that ship operates in accordance with policies and standards of chartered organization, Boy Scouts of America, and ship’s code and by-laws.
Responsible for completing the annual recharter.
Provide ship's officers with ideas and information.
Maintains liaison with the chartered organization.
Ship Committee Chairman (Chief Executive Officer)
Plan and conduct regular ship committee meetings.
Work closely with the Skipper to verify ship needs are being met.
Appoint ship committee positions as needed by the ship.
Conduct the annual recharter.
Approve adult applications for the ship committee.
Chartered Organization Representative
Maintain liaison with the chartered organization.
Approve adult applications for the chartered organization.

Secretary

Assist yeoman with youth membership records, advancement records, correspondence, and publicity.
Keep adult membership and training records.
Keep minutes of all committee meetings and other adult meetings that include group decisions and plans.
Remind the committee of the items that they agreed to do.

Treasurer

Assist purser with setting up bookkeeping procedures, bank deposits, and money handling methods.
Assist purser with balancing the checkbook, balancing activity accounts, and preparing the annual financial statement.

Equipment Advisor

Assist storekeeper with procuring equipment.
Assist storekeeper with maintenance and inventory procedures.

Activity Advisors

Assist appointed youth activity chairman with recruiting consultants, gathering equipment, or other duties as needed, for a one-time activity.

Consultant Advisor

Conduct the annual “Program Capability Inventory.”
Keep and expand a current list of approved consultants for youth activity chairman to draw from.

Consultants

Teach an activity or skill as requested by ship's officers.
Is not necessarily a committee member, but should be registered with the BSA.
