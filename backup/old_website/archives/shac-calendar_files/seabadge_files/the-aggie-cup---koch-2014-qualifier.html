The Aggie Cup - Koch 2014 Qualifier

NOTICE OF RACE:  2014 Koch Cup Southern Region Qualifying Regatta (“The Aggie Cup”)

Sea Scout Base Galveston

7511 Broadway

Galveston, TX


Back up Location: 

Texas A&M Galveston Sailing Team

Teichman Road Campus

8701 Teichman Rd, Galveston


September 28, 2013

Back up date:  October 19, 2013

 

The organizing authority for this event is the William I. Koch Race Committee.

1. RULES:

1.1. The regatta will be governed by rules as defined in The Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS) 2013-2016, the BSA Guide to Safe Scouting, the Sailing Instructions and this Notice of Race.

1.2. Certain rules may be changed; changes will appear in full in the Sailing Instructions.

1.3. The provisions of The Guide to Safe Scouting Article XII, Transportation, Boats, Section 6. to have on-board oars and rowlocks, fire extinguishers and lights are waived for the competing vessels only.

 

2. ELIGIBILITY & ENTRY:

2.1. The Event is open only to currently registered Sea Scouts in the Boy Scouts of America. All competitors must be at least thirteen (13) years of age and less than twenty-one (21) yearsof age on July 22, 2013.  All entrants must be active members of Sea Scout Units in the Southern Region.

2.2. This event is a qualifying regatta for The  2014 William I. Koch International Sea Scout Cup.  Complete information on eligibility for that event is found in the Official Notice of Race athttps://seascoutcup.org.

2.3. Entry is by two person teams (Skipper and Crew)

2.4. There is no entry fee.

 

3. ADVANCE REGISTRATION:

3.1. Teams shall register in advance.

3.2. Completed registration shall consist of a team entry form and, from each team member, aparental consent form and a Sea Scout Base Galveston Visitors Waiver.

3.3 Mail complete registration (Entry, Waiver and Parental Consent Forms to:

         Dan Wilson

        1811 Heather Cove Ct

        Houston, Texas 77062-8009

3.5 There is no entry fee.

 

4. SCHEDULE OF EVENTS:

Saturday, (date)

0830-0900 - Check-in at Sea Scout Base Galveston office

0900-0930 - Competitors Meeting and Boat Rigging

1015 - Warning Signal for first race.

 

5. SAILING INSTRUCTIONS:

Sailing Instructions will be available at check-in and at the competitors meeting.


6. SCORING:

6.1 A maximum of eight races are scheduled, of which one completed race shall constitute a series. If six or more races are sailed, a boat’s worst score shall be discarded.

6.2. The regatta will be scored using the Low Point system of Appendix A, except as modified by the sailing instructions.

6.3 Protests hearings, if any, will take place 30 minutes after the race committee boat returns to the launching platform.

 

9. BOATS:

Racing will be on FJ's owned by the Seabase   Crews will change boats after lunch.

 

10. VENUE:

Racing will take place on Offatts Bayou south of the Sea Scout Base Galveston.

 

11. SAFETY:

11.1. A US Coast Guard approved personal flotation device shall be worn, properly fastened, at all times while launching and when sailing. Failure to comply with this rule can result in disqualification as deemed appropriate by the protest committee. A boat retiring from a race shall notify a Race Committee vessel before leaving the course, or when that is impossible, at the Sail Team Clubhouse immediately after arrival ashore.

11.2. Competitors must be accompanied by a parent(s) or registered BSA adult leaders as required by BSA Guide to Safe Scouting, Youth Protection Requirements.

 

12. MEALS:

Lunch on the day of the race will be provided at no charge to entrants, parents and spectators. Participants are responsible for their own food at all other times.

 

13. DIRECTIONS:

13.1. Sea Scout Base Galveston from Houston and points north and west: Take I-45 south to Galveston Island. After the I-45 causeway onto Galveston Island, take the Second Exit, 1B - 71st Street. Proceed approximately 0.2 miles or eight telephone poles to Sea Scout Base Galveston entrance, 7511 Broadway, Galveston, TX.

 

14. HOUSING:

Limited berth space is available by prior arrangement at Sea Scout Base Galveston (SSBG). Contact Dan Wilson at skipperdan@comcast.net for details.

 

16. ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:

For additional information, contact Dan Wilson at Skipperdan@comcast.net. Entrants and guests should bring their own portable chairs, umbrellas, etc.   Limited berthing may be available at  Sea Scout Base Galveston. Should that become an option, it will be announced at least a month prior to the race.

 

17. DISCLAIMER OF LIABILITY:

Sailing is an activity that has an inherent risk of damage and injury. Competitors in this event are participating entirely at their own risk. See RRS 4, Decision to Race.
